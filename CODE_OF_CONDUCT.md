# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

SIPMan aims to be a practical, respectful project for people building and
testing SIP systems. We want discussion here to stay professional, direct, and
welcoming, especially when debugging difficult production-style failures.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but accept corrections when your impact differs from your
  intent.
- Keep criticism focused on code, behavior, documentation, and technical tradeoffs.
- Share enough context for others to understand and reproduce issues.
- Be careful with customer, carrier, account, and network information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Posting credentials, private keys, customer logs, or sensitive SIP traces.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or pull requests after maintainers ask you to stop.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe or hostile.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions,
project chat, release channels, and other SIPMan community spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, close issues or pull requests, block
participants, or take other reasonable action to protect the project community.
Enforcement should be proportionate, documented where appropriate, and focused
on restoring a constructive environment.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the project maintainer through their
GitHub profile contact details or another private channel you already have with
the maintainer. Do not publish sensitive personal, customer, or security details
in a public issue.

Maintainers will review reports in good faith and keep reporter information as
private as practical.
